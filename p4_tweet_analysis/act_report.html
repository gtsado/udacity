Background: The analysis that this report refers to is based on analysis done on tweets done by Twitter user WeRateDogs. WeRateDogs is a twitter account that rates peoples dogs with a humours comment about each dog.

Once the data was gathered, assessed, and cleaned, I was interested in seeing what, if any, interesting insights could be extracted from the dataset.

I found that the most common dog breed tweeted about by far was the Golden Retriever with 139 of total tweets being about Golden Retrievers. Additionally, when I looked into the average rating of each dog breed, I found that Golden Retrievers had the the highest average rating amongst the top 10 most common dog tweets. Next, I was curious to know how/if favorites or retweets of WeRateDogs tweets were affected by the month that the tweets were tweeted. There seems to be the same general pattern in that from March till about June, WeRateDogs experiences an increase in the number of favorites and retweets of his tweets. There is, however, a noticeable sharp decline in the number of retweets and favorites between August and November but when you look at the data separated by year, we learn that the reason for this is because only 2016 has favorite/retweet data for a full calendar year.

What is the relationship between favorites and retweets?¶

Lastly, I was interested in seeing the relationship between favorites and retweets of WeRateDogs tweets. As I suspected, there appears to be a strong positive correlation between favorites and retweets. Whats pretty cool to see though, I how the popularity of WeRateDogs tweets changes as the years go by (as evidenced by the number of favorites and retweets being increasing for each year).
